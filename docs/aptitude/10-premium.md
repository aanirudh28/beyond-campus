# 10 — Premium & The Funnel

## The line (never crossed)

**Free forever, no exceptions:** every question, every explanation, every mock,
every analytics view, mastery map, readiness scores, challenges, circles,
company pages, weekly report. If a student never pays, they still get the full
learning machine. This is the moat and the marketing.

**Metered (cost-accrual only, mirrors Tracker):** AI Tutor chat ("explain this
differently / where did I go wrong in MY reasoning") — 5/month free, unlimited
with the existing ₹299 Pro bundle (extend `syncProEntitlement`; one flag serves
both products). Haiku cost makes this honest metering, not artificial scarcity.

**Paid (humans only, existing ladder — no new SKUs):**
- ₹549 strategy call (credited toward cohorts within 30 days, per existing model)
- ₹1,750 Internship Cohort · ₹2,500 Placement Cohort
- ₹299 Resource Pack (now also carries AI-unlimited in aptitude)

## Why aptitude feeds the cohorts (the honest theory)

Aptitude clears the *test*. The cohort clears the *human being* — targeting,
resume, outreach, GD, interviews. These are genuinely different products, so
the upsell is truthful: "we got you past the SHL test; the interview is a
different game." A student with a 90-day practice history, a 300-point rating
climb, and a readiness score they trust does not need persuading — they need
an invitation at the right moment.

## The three surfaces (all of them, no others)

1. **Post-mock report footer** — "Want a human to walk this report with you?
   ₹549 call, credited to any cohort." Contextual, dismissible, appears from
   rung-2 mocks onward.
2. **Plateau nudge** — rating flat ±15 for 3+ weeks with ≥10 sets: "Plateaus
   at your level are usually strategy, not effort. This is the exact problem
   cohort mentors solve." Shown at most once per plateau.
3. **Quiet nav item** — "Mentorship" in More; static page, cohort details,
   Results Wall testimonials filtered to aptitude-track alumni.

Rules: no popups, no timers, no fake seat counts, no interrupting a session,
ever. Track conversion per surface (doc 06 events); expected honest range
1–3% of weekly-active → paid something within 6 months. If a surface converts
by annoying (high click, high churn-after), kill it.

## Anti-goals

- No "premium question packs" — the moment one question costs money, the
  "best free platform anywhere" claim dies and IndiaBix wins on trust.
- No ads. Ever. (Destroys the study surface and signals cheapness.)
- No B2B college licensing distraction in year 1 (TPO dashboards stay a free
  growth loop; monetizing them is a different company).
